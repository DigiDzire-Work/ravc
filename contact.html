Frequntly Ask Questions

Solar panels work using photovoltaic (PV) cells that convert sunlight directly into electricity. When sunlight hits the PV cells, it knocks electrons loose, creating an electric current. This current is then converted from DC to AC electricity by an inverter, which can be used to power your home.​

Most solar panels have a lifespan of 25 to 30 years. Over time, their efficiency slightly decreases (typically about 0.5% per year), but they can continue to produce electricity well beyond their warranty period.​

Solar panels require minimal maintenance. Regular cleaning to remove dust, dirt, and debris, and periodic inspections to ensure all components are functioning properly, are usually sufficient. Most panels are designed to withstand various weather conditions and don’t need much upkeep.

The amount you can save depends on several factors, including your location, electricity rates, and the size of your solar system. On average, homeowners save between 50% and 80% on their electricity bills after switching to solar. Over 20-25 years, this can add up to tens of thousands of dollars in savings. Additionally, federal and state incentives can reduce the upfront cost, further enhancing your savings​.
